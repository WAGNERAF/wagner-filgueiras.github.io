Greenville City News

With temperatures rising and powerful thunderstorms a threat most summer afternoons, the potential for power outages is on the minds of residents across the Upstate. About 17,000 Duke Energy customers in the state lost power after Monday evening's storm, for example, which was reduced to 7,000 by 6 a.m. Tuesday, said Duke Energy spokesman Ryan Mosier. All it takes is a downed tree, a lightning strike or overstressed equipment for a community to be without power for hours with no access to air conditioning in the sweltering heat.
